# Cinematic Interface — RealityKit 4

**Tagline:** The most beautiful way to talk to your home AI.

---

## What it is

The You-Sir Juan™ iOS app is built on RealityKit 4 and Metal — Apple's native 3D rendering stack — delivering an interface that feels closer to a cinematic experience than a mobile app. Physics-based motion, Z-space depth, live lighting, and spatial audio combine to make the interface feel alive.

This is not decoration. It is the intentional design choice that makes the product feel premium, personal, and worth having on your wall.

---

## Why it matters

The interface is the first thing a family member sees every time they approach the kiosk. It should feel like walking into their space. Not opening an app. The RealityKit rendering stack makes that possible in a way no web framework or cross-platform tool can match.

The technical benchmark: 120fps on M-series iPad hardware, Metal-optimized rendering, sub-5% battery impact. Fast enough that the UI disappears and the experience takes over.

---

## What you see

**The Associate Presence**
The home screen centers on the Associate Agent's visual presence — a RealityView scene with a floating accent-colored orb that pulses gently, responds to device tilt via CoreMotion, and casts soft light in the member's paradigm color. This is the visual anchor of the Associate relationship.

**Z-space depth**
UI elements are layered in 3D space — closer elements feel immediate, background elements recede. Navigation transitions use physics springs, not flat slides. The interface has weight and momentum.

**Paradigm rendering**
Every member's visual world is rendered at the Metal level — accent colors, point lighting, and material properties all derive from the member's paradigm tokens. Switching members doesn't just change colors; it changes the physical mood of the scene.

## visionOS

The You-Sir Juan™ visionOS target ships alongside the iOS app. On Apple Vision Pro, the Associate Agent lives in an ImmersiveSpace — a full spatial scene that fills the user's field of view. The same design system, the same backend, rendered for spatial computing.
